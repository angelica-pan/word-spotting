Overview:

Professor Jesse Harris from the Linguistics Department at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) is conducting a research study. You are being offered the opportunity to complete this study for SONA credit. Your participation in this research study is voluntary. Please complete this study in a quiet room, away from music, television, or other distractions. Please use headphones if possible.

Note: Once you move past this page, please complete the experiment until you reach the end. If you cannot complete the experiment in one sitting before the deadline, you can cancel the time slot on SONA.

You may complete this experiment for credit only once.

Description:

This internet study is being conducted to determine how native speakers attend to spoken language. During the experiment, you'll listen for a word provided before each sentence and press a button as soon as you hear it.

You may see questions about what you listened to after some sentences, so be sure to pay close attention. A sample is shown in the box below:

Listen for: CATTLE
Listen to: After the long ride, the cowboy rounded up the cattle and took a big drink from a cold bottle of water.
Answer question: WHAT DID THE COWBOY DRINK?
MILK          WATER


Before you begin the experiment, you will be guided through a few practice items to get a feel for what the specific task is.

Requirements:
